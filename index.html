Forró [fɔˈʁɔ]

Forró is a musical style and couple dance from the Northeastern region of Brazil. It is now known and popular throughout Brazil and increasingly attracting more and more dance enthusiasts around the world. The instruments typically dominant in traditional forró, also called forró pé-de-serra, are the zabumba (Brazilian bass drum), the sanfona (Brazilian accordion) and the triangle. The musical genre includes various rhythms such as baião, xote, xaxado or arrasta-pé.

Amadia

Hello! My name is Amadia and over the years, dancing forró has become one of my favourite activities. I was born in Vienna and have lived in the Netherlands, Scotland, Belgium and Brazil. When I was young my mom introduced me to belly dancing and Middle Eastern folk dances. Ever since I have continued dancing, while discovering new rhythms and styles. Along the years, I have danced salsa, kizomba, bachata and funk.

Yerko

Hello! my name is Yerko, and my story with Forró started with the idea of watching a samba or bosa nova concert in a bar. To my surprise, there was a cozy environment of people dancing to an unknown rhythm to me. It appears that the band was Banda Caburé, and one of the dancers told me that the music is called Forró (now the DJ of Forró no Vinyl). Since then, I have learned how to dance it thanks to lovely people. Now, I would like to share the dance with you.
